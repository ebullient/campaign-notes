# The Gulthias Tree

You all made rough plans over breakfast at Falcon's Lodge with Reidoth and Xanth. You gathered your things, and hiked the two hours to Woodland Manse without incident. The grounds were quiet. Deserted. Malevolent. Ominous. The tree looked whole. Far more healthy than you expected, given the damage you did to it the last time you saw it. 

There was some quick last minute planning, and then Reidoth summoned two earth elementals, who loosened the earth around the tree and the well. Then Kyle attatched the rope to the tree. Brocc was mammothed, and the rope was then hitched to him. Brocc gave a mighty heave, and the walls of the well split as the tree began to move in the direction it was pulled. 

Xanth suggested that Reidoth use stone shape to push the tree out of the ground, and (with haste and care), Reidoth was able to touch an unbroken bit of the well wall on the far side of the tree. The summoned earth elementals still lingered in the ground, and they helped guide and propel the tree using the stone shaped by Reidoth for that purpose. The tree flew up and over the courtyard wall, raining dirt and small stones everywhere. 

Once the rest of the party left the courtyard to deal with the the remains of the tree, Reidoth thanked and dismissed the earth elementals before casting moonbeam on a patch of disturbed earth in the ruined courtyard. He used the next few minutes to systematically move the beam across all of the loose, overturned earth to burn any small roots that may have broken off.

Outside, Grizzlebear took the rope off the tree, and started a wall of fire burning around the main trunk of the tree. The rest of the party picked up loose branches, or hacked off any parts of the tree not surrounded by fire, and tossed them in. By the time Reidoth was done systematically drenching the courtyard with radiant damage from a moonbeam spell, the wall of fire had reduced the rest of the tree to fine ash.

In the midst of all of this activity and back and forth, someone had the wherewithal to mend the rope of mending and give it to Grizzlebear.

As you all gathered outside of the Manse and the fire finally died down, the ominous air about the Manse dissipated. All of you were able to feel the change in mood as the influence of the Gulthias tree was broken. 

You did a bit more poking about inside the manor. Kyle showed Reidoth the mud drawings on the floor, and Reidoth explained a bit about the the symbols of Talos and the usually reclusive anchorites,  noting that this activity (the Gulthias Tree in particular) is a little unusual for anchorites, who were usually happy enough summoning lightning and destruction during storm season, or wishing for catastrophe to disrupt seasonal festivals. 

Reidoth also remembered the Wizard who lived in the Manse, and did some poking around of his own. He discovered (and kept) a Staff of Birdcalls.

Generally satisfied with the change in mood and that you had nothing else to discover, you made the 2-hour return hike back to the Lodge. Given the size and alertness of the party, the forest stayed out of your way, and you made good time, reaching the Lodge in early afternoon.

Immeral accepted Falcon's gift of the Boots of Elvenkind. Riardon broke out the Lute of Illusions, and entertained all of you as you spent the evening with Falcon, Corwin, and Pell. After an evening filled with stories and music and good food, you all went to your "usual" places for a long rest.
