# All is Well
  
After clearing the Redbrands out of their hideout, the party returned to Phandalin in the late evening with 3 farm boys, a meek goblin, and a snobbishly obsequious wizard in tow. They made a beeline for Harbin Wester’s house, and Riardon knocked on the door. A small eye-slot opened, “Who’s there? I’m not coming out to be snatched up by dragons.”

Riardon summarized their encounters in the hideout, with Gildas chipping in commentary, “My good gentlemen, there must be some misunderstanding. There may have been a few unfortunate events along the way, but my guests were always treated well.“ 

Brocc snorted, and rummaged through the paperwork he and Droop were carrying. “Nice try, darling Glasstaff,” he said as he passed the letter from the Black Spider to Harbin through the slot in the door. 

Harbin mumbled the words as he read the note. “Oh dear. How long this has been going on? And someone is informing this.. this... Black Spider.. of what is going on in Phandalin? Who would do such a thing? Why would they?”

Kyle poked Glasstaff with his own staff. “Ouch! That was rude”, he complained. Kyle raised an eyebrow and loomed. “Alright, alright, “ sighed Glasstaff. He cleared his throat nervously, “The Black Spider is a drow looking for old magical artifacts now that old mines are being opened up again. I was ... sent ... here, as most materials and artifacts pulled from nearby sites come here before going north to Neverwinter or south to Leilon on the way to Waterdeep. Even east-west traffic on the Triboar trail stops in Phandalin regularly.” He shrugged, “All water under the bridge now, right? What do you say, gentlemen? I think dinner sounds excellent. Roasted meat, or maybe tender lamb, and some wine... You all are welcome to join me, of course.” His winning smile faltered, dashed against the stern expressions around him.

Kyle looked at the door, “What should we do with him, Townmaster?” 

Some muffled sounds, like things being moved around came from behind the door, and then the distinct clicks of several latches and locks opening. The door creaked open, and Harbin stepped timidly out of his house. He gave the whole party a good look-over and jumped when he registered Droop, standing there looking forlorn, miserable, and completely harmless. "GAH! What is that?"

Riardon covered John's exasperated sigh, "It's a goblin, Harbin. This poor thing was essentially a slave to, and perhaps also the preferred punching bag for, 3 bugbears. Thel told us some of how he was treated while he was the guest of the Redbrands." Riardon looked over at Glasstaff, who averted his eyes and muttered again about finding some dinner and wine.

Harbin took a shaky breath, and looked beyond Droop to take in Daniel, Seamus, and George. "And who are those three? They're dressed like those bandits, if I understood your last report."

"They were Redbrands, Townmaster," said Brocc, "dreamy farm boys lured away by promises of wealth and riches. No mention was made to them about how that wealth would be obtained." Brocc leveled a disapproving glare at Glasstaff, who answered, "My fine gentlemen, such accusations! Really, we should just go have dinner. We'll all feel better after some dinner and wine, right?" He made another attempt at a conspiratorial grin, which failed to influence anyone to take his side.

"Right," Harbin said, seeming to gather himself together. "There are a few holding cells in the Townmaster's Hall, let's get this over with." With a last furtive look up and down the small road in front of his house, he stepped out into the road and headed toward the Townmaster's Hall. Kyle took pity on the cowardly Townmaster and stepped up to walk at his side. Harbin relaxed very slightly. "Who do you usually have keep watch, Townmaster?" Kyle asked.

Startled, Harbin almost missed a step, "Oh," he said, "we have a few guards in Phandalin, but not many. And they are mostly volunteer, and have other jobs besides."

"Those boys," Kyle said, "they are all looking for something to do now, you know. That's why they left their homes in the first place. I don't think we can offer them anything regular, and we certainly can't pay them. Can you think of anything they might be able to do?"

Harbin looked thoughtful, "They can start, I suppose, by keeping watch now, while I rustle up some of the other townsfolk to help. I wonder if any of them would be willing to give Maccus a hand at the Smithy? And I think you said Thel and his family have returned, right? They might not mind another young man around the shop for awhile. I might ask Daran if he could use some help, too. Daran is an old adventurer, he might know how to settle these three." Harbin walked a few more steps in cautious silence, "what do you plan to do with .. with the Goblin?"

"With Droop?", said Kyle, "I don't know. We hadn't really made plans beyond coming back here. What are you thinking?"

"Well," Harbin said, "I guess I'm feeling unexpected sympathy for the creature. He looks as scared as I feel. My door has a lot of locks on it, and goodness knows I spend too much time alone. Maybe I can teach a goblin how to play chess." He huffed a laugh. "I'm worried about my brother, too. He's off in those confounded woods. I haven't heard anything from him. It's about time to send supplies out to his camp, anyway. Would you.. would you take them to him? I trust you and your friends to make sure my brother is alright."

Kyle gave the Townmaster a friendly pat on the shoulder, as they turned to enter the hall, "We can do that, Harbin, don't fret."

There was some shuffling and shifting and sorting, as Gildas Glasstaff was pushed into a holding cell after his pockets were emptied. Harbin claimed his staff for the time being. "Until Mabina comes back, you know. I don't think you have any wizards in your party, and I'm pretty sure I know what this staff is, and it will only work for wizards." He cast a glance at Daniel, George, and Seamus. "Sir Monsterdrinker here says you three were caught up in this scheme without knowing what you were getting into?" They all nodded, shamefacedly. "Well then, you lot stay just outside the door for a bit, and I'll send a few folk here to talk to you. I know what you look like now, so don't scamper off. I'm taking the key to this cell with me, and I've had help in the past rigging things so I know if and when a cell door has been opened. If you let Glasstaff out, I will know."  He stopped then and, surprising everyone, crouched down to meet Droop's eyes. "I think I heard them call you Droop, is that right?"

Droop started back a little, but relaxed at Harbin's words, "The bugbears and things called me Droop, yes, they did. My real name is Drusz, but they laughed at me, and beat me, and ... and ... then they called me Droop instead." The goblin was shorter than Brocc, and looked tired and sad. 

"Well, Drusz," said Harbin, "would you like to come back to my house with me? We can have tea and try to play chess. If the dragon comes, I'll make sure there is room under the bed for you, too." The goblin looked ready to cry, but managed a nod instead. Harbin stood and looked at Kyle, "I may regret this, once he recovers, but for the moment, I'm satisfied. Would you 6 split up to deliver a few messages before retiring?"

The party agreed, as it was the least they could do to see George, Seamus, and Daniel settled. Harbin went upstairs and jotted a few notes. The party split up and delivered notes to Maccus Strongarm at the Smithy, to Thel Dendrar and his family (who were safely home and brimming with thanks), to Daran Edermath at his orchard, and to Linene Graywind, at the Lionshield Coster. Interestingly, Harbin sent nothing to Halia Thornton.

After all messages were delivered, with Droop/Drusz, George, Seamus, and Daniel as settled as the could be, the party returned to the Stonehill Inn for some well-deserved rest. Kyle let the party know he'd already agreed that they would take supplies out to the Townmaster's brother's camp, "Assuming we return with Tibor's signature, the Townmaster has agreed to pay us 100gp. While he asked me if we would do this because he trusts us, he isn't asking us to do this without compensation." There wasn't much grumbling about it, as the Townmaster had been more than helpful with the crew of misfits they had brought back from the Redbrand's hideout. 

They all settled down for a lovely night's sleep, and were due to visit Barthen's Provisions in the morning, to pick up the supplies and plan their route.
