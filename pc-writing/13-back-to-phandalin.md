# Back to Phandalin

You bid a fond(er) farewell to Falcon, and thanked him for his hospitality.

Walking through the forest, with the Ranger with favored terrain in the front and the high-perception Druid following behind, was uneventful, but between the edge of the forest and the road, a large Ettin played with his death-dog, "Bitey". 
You (collectively, and almost simultaneously) managed to find a new stick for the dog. Kyle used a lot of strength to hurl the new stick, which Bitey chased, dove after, pounced on, and then happily bounded away with. The Ettin was happy that his dog was happy, which left you free and clear to make your way to the road.

You camped a little after sundown, once the booming voice of the Ettin and Bitey's barking had faded into the distance.

You were visited by Pixies overnight. Some shoes were tied together, your hair was knotted, a pack strap was unstitched... Nothing was damaged beyond quick repair, and nothing was taken. You may or may not have heard a giggle while disentangling yourselves in the morning.

After breaking camp, you walked until the road to Phandalin split from the Triboar Trail, and stopped there for lunch. You had the weirdest experience as you were packing up: a misty/foggy cloud literally blew right through you. You resumed your journey, happy to be nearing its end. You noticed that a cloud had formed on the road in front of you. As you approached, that cloud solidified into Mabina's wagon. She greeted you warmly, surprised to meet you on the road (she probably expected to find you in town).

Mabina shared some stories from her journey, which seemed far reaching, almost too far reaching to have happened just since you last saw her, but Riardon sensed only honesty and openness coming from Mabina. Beyond that, she picked up a few magic items along the way with specific members of the party in mind. You get the feeling that what she traded the bauble for changed her circumstances in a significant (and positive) way.

As you traded news, she confirmed the resettlement of a town along the highroad (a little south of the Triboar Trail intersection). She mentioned trouble with sea trade along the coast, and you tied that to the contents of Fheralai's letters.

You parted ways in front of the Stonehill Inn: Mabina headed to the town green with her wagon, and you headed to ... your house. John made improvements while you were gone (8 days total). Despite never having slept here before, it did feel like "home" (though perhaps not as fancy as the lordling Kyle was used to). There are beds for everyone, and you each find one that is just right.
