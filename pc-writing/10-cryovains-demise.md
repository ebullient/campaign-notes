# Cryovain's Demise
![cryovain](/[assets]/attachments/cryovain.jpeg#portrait)

You left Phandalin, and hiked towards Icespire Peak for 25 miles. You passed one completely frozen Orc Ice Popsicle along the way.

[LONG REST]

You finished the last 5 miles of approach to the mountain, and then began to follow a narrow but stable (and also very windy) trail that wound up the mountainside to the gatehouse of the fortress.

You faced down a group of mercenaries, who'd staked out turf in the gatehouse of the fortress, and were waiting for Cryovain to leave so they could raid the dragon's horde. You took the leader down, and seriously injured another. With the leader unconscious and bleeding out, they were more willing to negotiate. You agreed they could have all the treasure, you just wanted to kill the dragon. With two of their party seriously wounded, they agreed, and withdrew into a room they were using as barracks to tend their wounded.

You proceeded across the bridge from the gatehouse into the old War Leader's fortress, and cleared your way through all of the rooms finding only meager leavings here and there. You made your way onto the roof, and found Cryovain asleep. A successful sneak attack round and a bombardment of fireballs allowed Cryovain to get only one really good hit on any of you. She flew across the roof to get away, but not far enough. 

On the way to finishing her off, you aggravated two Ice Mephits (one attacked and then fled out of reach; you attacked, stunned, and killed the other). There were other Ice Mephits on the roof, but they remained undisturbed (and seemed content not to challenge you as you left them alone).

You gathered teeth and claws and scales. So many scales (and no horses to carry them on). You left Cryovain as a bloody shredded heap on the roof, and wound your way back through the fortress. 

[Short rest for Brocc, Riardon, and Acronix]

You paused a few times to make sure you'd left no interesting parts of the fortress unexplored, and stopped only to let the Reavers know that you'd killed the dragon. Runa, the one with the short black hair and the crossbow, was downright floored that Grizzlebear gave her a dragon scale. She was left wondering what cruelty awaits her next.

You put 5 miles between yourselves and the mountain before you settled for the night, which was plenty of walking distance to shake off the cold of the mountain. When not on watch, you all slept the sleep of people who just killed a dragon.

[LONG REST]

You woke and began the rest of the trek back to Phandalin (still in a jolly good mood, because, HEY! you killed a dragon!). You did notice something flying overhead, but carried on trying to decide what to do with all the dragon parts you've gathered. You walked on for a bit more before you noticed the shadow fly over again. It was the Manticore, but rather than shoot first and talk later, she swooped down to talk first. Whatever plan she had was completely derailed when you told her the dragon was dead. She flew off again almost immediately (probably to see if you were lying).

You walked on for a bit more, and as has happened before, the earth rumbled a spare moment before an Ankheg erupted from below Grizzlebear. Its surprise attack was effective, and Grizzlebear was grappled before he even knew there was something to fight. In the end, the ankheg was alone and it picked a party to attack that was perhaps a little too big for it. It was a matter of moments before it was stabbed to death, at which point it rained acid on whoever was in the vicinity (Kyle, Immeral, and Grizzlebear).

You paid a little more attention and were a bit more focused on your surroundings for the remainder of your trip back to Phandalin. You all were tired, and some still a little hurt, and you really didn't want to spend another night outside. You made it back into town just as night was falling. With visions of what to do with your spoils hovering in your eyes, you did things a little out of the usual and went to the Stonehill Inn first. Toblen and Amelia were glad to see you, even at the late hour, and walked you to rooms you realize you've been staying in off and on for over a month. Some of you wonder if you should think about getting your own place, others don't worry about anything other than which side of their pillow is colder.

The last few words you exchange before you all fall asleep, is general agreement to see Harbin in the morning, and then find out as much as you can about what all of this dragon stuff is worth.
