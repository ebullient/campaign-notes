# A Rough Time in Phandalin

Our adventuring party returned to Phandalin from the south. They passed the Phandalin Miner’s Exchange, which had its usual level of traffic in the late afternoon as miners returned from the field to register new claims or exchange raw materials.

They continued north, in the general direction of the inn, and paused as there was an interesting cart pulled up near Harbin Wester’s house (visible just down the rutted side-road from the Townmaster’s Hall). 

![Interesting cart](/[assets]/attachments/mabina-wagon.png#full-width)

Curious, they headed that way to see what was what. A woman bustled around the cart. She didn’t seem old, or young, really. The layers and hood of her robe made it hard to figure out her age.

![Mabina](/[assets]/attachments/mabina.jpg#portrait)

After some conversation, Kyle and Grizzlebear learned that her name was Mabina. She had some kind of friendship or kinship with Harbin from an event that happened long ago. Because of that event, Harbin trusted trading magic items with her. Magic items were tricky to trade, as deception of some kind or another was usually involved.

Grizzlebear had a Hat of Wizardry (from Gnomengarde) that none in the party were able to use. He knew if he gave it to Harbin Wester, he would get 50 gold pieces. While nice, there wasn’t anywhere in Phandalin that he could buy another magic item, so he asked Mabina what she would trade for it. A hat, a horn, some boots and a rope were visible. Mabina offered any of those things, and Grizzlebear opted for the Boots of False Tracks, which allow a humanoid wearer to leave tracks belonging to a different humanoid behind, “A heavy mountain dwarf could leave the comparatively delicate footprints of a high elf, for example,” Mabina explained.

Kyle also looked at the items, and opted to exchange the Potion of Clairvoyance and Potion of Animal Friendship he was carrying for a Rope of Mending. “Provided you keep all of the parts, you can cut this rope into as many pieces as you like (of whatever length), and then, once you’re done using them, line the ends up so they are touching, say the command word, and the rope will mend itself into a single coil again. Nifty,” said Mabina.

They kept talking, and Mabina asked them if they knew anything for sure about whether or not there was a dragon in the area. She used magic to hide herself, her cart, and her mule from would-be attackers, but a dragon might require some extra effort. Kyle was able to confirm that it was a dragon, and further, it was a young white dragon, as the party had a not-so-successful encounter with it already. 

In return, Mabina warned of strange magic in Neverwinter Wood. She avoided entering the forest, and not only because she was in a wagon pulled by a mule. Navigators tended to get lost in the forest, even skilled navigators. Most humans stayed by the river, because it was harder to get lost that way. While she never met him, she heard that there was a noble from Neverwinter that had a Hunting Lodge in the woods along the river’s edge. Someone in town probably knew more than that, as the woods aren’t that far away.

After thinking a bit, Kyle decided that, as a dealer in magical artifacts, she might be hiding something a bit rarer in her wagon. It was a risk, but he brought out the Splendiferous Bauble. Mabina was fooled by it. She took it and, as compelled by the bauble, went into the wagon to retrieve the most valuable item she carried, a rare Handy Haversack. Kyle took the backpack and the party took their leave of Mabina, who resumed fussing about her cart as she prepared to leave town. 

As they were nearby, they knocked on Harbin’s door. A small eye-slot opened, “Who’s there? I’m not coming out to be snatched up by dragons.”

Kyle and Grizzlebear, speaking for the party, explained the goings on in Gnomengarde. Harbin was only somewhat interested. He was more curious about whether or not they’d brought any magical items back. While miffed that they hadn’t brought the hat to him, he couldn’t blame Kyle and Grizzlebeard for trading the Hat of Wizardry with Mabina, as that was what he was going to do anyway.

Brocc spoke up at that point, and asked if Harbin had anything else that needed to be done. Harbin mentioned two things: It was about time to get more supplies out to his brother’s logging camp (always a treacherous endeavor that required an escort). Also, while his time outside was as short as it could be (going between the Townmaster’s hall and his house, and posting things on the board), he’d heard enough to think something was wrong in Phandalin, but he didn’t know what. He offered 50 gold pieces if the party could come back the next day to fill him in.

The party bade farewell to Harbin, who closed the eye-slit in his door with a snap, and resumed their trek up the main road toward the Stonehill Inn. It was just after dinner time when they entered the Inn’s common room. They used money from their common fund to get a decent meal and rooms for the night. 

John and Kyle spoke for a bit with Amelia, who told them something had happened to Thel, the local woodcarver. She was really worried about him and his family, as they hadn’t been seen for a few days. He’d tossed some ne’er-do-wells out of his shop for threatening his wife and daughters. Three days ago he’d disappeared. Two days ago, his wife and daughters disappeared, too. Phandalin was a small town, but it had its share of bandits nipping around the edges of town. It was close enough to both the Highroad and the Triboar Trail to make it a frequent restocking stop for travelers and caravans going to and from the city of Neverwinter. Nothing like the disappearance of Thel and his family had ever happened before.

Pip, Amelia and Toblen’s son, came into the common room after running a few last-minute errands for his parents. He told his mother, John, and Kyle that his friend Carp found a secret tunnel! Carp almost got caught by the people using it, but Carp is good at hiding, so they didn’t see him.

Kyle and John excused themselves, and returned to the meal with the rest of the party, sharing what they’d learned from Amelia and Pip. As it was late and they’d had enough excitement fighting orcs on their way back to Phandalin, the party resolved to check out what was going on in the morning. They headed upstairs and used the table in the common room to sort through their belongings, moving extra and spare equipment into the capacious Haversack, as that lightened the load on everyone. Items rearranged, they retired for the night.

When they went downstairs for breakfast, there were a few others in the common room starting their day. 

* Lanar, a miner, was fixated on the increased presence of Orcs in the area, which just made his job harder.  “I am seeing Orc raiders everywhere. Someone needs to do something about it.” While he looked a little startled by Grizzlebeard’s presence in the party, he seemed somewhat mollified when Grizzlebeard let him know that the party killed 11 orcs yesterday. “Well, that’s a start, I guess.”

* Freda, a weaver, and an old farmer named Narth were sitting together by the fire chatting over their morning meal. Narth fretted a bit over Sister Garaele, who came back from a trip out of town wounded and exhausted. Freda grumbled about a group of ruffians that have been harassing her shop, and all of the other shops as far as she knew. “The Miner’s Exchange is the only business they’re leaving alone. Halia Thornton is a formidable opponent. I don’t think they want to cross paths with her.”

* Elsa, the barmaid, came down into the common room after tidying things upstairs. She gave the party a once over, “Oooh, are you all adventurers? You should go find Daran Edermath. He was an adventurer once upon a time. He started his orchard when he got tired of adventuring. The stories he tells sometimes.. “

After finishing their breakfast, the party decided to visit Halia at the Phandalin Miner’s Exchange.

The Miner’s Exchange was fairly empty that morning. Halia was pouring over records at a large table covered with maps at the back of the room. John tried to stifle an involuntary sneeze as the party entered the room, drawing Halia’s attention. “Can I help you?”, she asked. Her eyes gave the party a shrewd once over, giving everyone an immediate understanding of what Freda meant. 

After a somewhat disorganized attempt at asking questions, Halia quirked an eyebrow, “I know who you mean. They call themselves ‘The Redbrands’. They have a base of some kind in, under, or near the old Tresendar Manor. They’ve been harassing shops for weeks now, though they lie low between activities for just long enough that people don’t get determined or organized enough to flush them out.

“Harbin is a coward, and he wouldn’t recognize a group like this if they managed to pull him out of his own house.” She paused, and gave Brocc a considering look. “I’ll give you 100 gold pieces if you eliminate their leader, someone they call Glasstaff, and bring me any correspondence you find in their quarters.”  

Riardon was watching her closely, and he had a strong suspicion that Halia wanted that leader removed for her own reasons. He wondered how much more she knew that she wasn’t sharing. 

“If you want to find them,” she added, “there are always a few at the Sleeping Giant tap room. You could start there.”

The party filed out of the Miner’s Exchange, and went up the road to knock on Harbin Wester’s door. “What?” he barked as he opened eye-slit in his door. Brocc, acting as spokesman, shared what they’d learned from Amelia, Pip, and Halia. Harbin grunted, “Well, what are these Redbrands then, humans? beasts?” Brocc confessed that they didn’t know. Harbin laughed harshly, and pushed 10 gold pieces under his door, “I’ll give more when you can answer a few more questions.”

The party decided to head to the Sleeping Giant taproom, a ramshackle building at the far end of the road that lead to Tresendar Manor. Six armed people, including a silver dragonborn, walking down the road naturally attract attention, including the sullen stares of the four scruffy, armed men occupying the taproom’s covered porch.

“Well, well,” said one, “what have we here?”
“A whole pack of little puppies, looks like”, said another.
“What do you want, little puppies, .. did you come here to bark at us?”, said a third. 
The fourth opted not to speak at all, and spat at Brocc’s feet instead.

Riardon looked at Brocc, and said, “Time to eat these chicken nuggets, yeah?”

A fight ensued, and the ruffians lost. Badly. A bystander would wonder why the ruffians started the fight to begin with, as they were already outnumbered. The last remaining tried to flee toward the Manor, but was also brought down before making more than a few steps in that direction. With no survivors to question, the party looted the bodies, taking the red cloaks, two decent daggers, and all of their pocket change (15 silver pieces in total). The clubs and shortswords were not worth keeping. 

Kyle, Grizzlebear, Immeral, and Riardon each picked up a body, and the whole party went back to Harbin Wester’s house.

“What now?” Harbin groused after opening the eye-slit in his door. 

Kyle, Grizzlebear, Immeral, and Riardon all gestured to the bodies they were carrying. “Redbrands, Harbin. Some are human, at least. We found them where Halia said we would,” Kyle said. 

“She knows more about them than she told us”, Riardon added.

“Oh for heaven’s sake, did you have to kill them all?” Harbin’s already worried voice sounded even more worried. “The last thing we need is retaliation from an armed group of bandits. Well, I did promise more gold if you brought me more information, and you have, so here is another 20 gold pieces. I think there is more to discover about this group, but please don’t cause any more trouble! There is a burial area out beyond the Town Green, south of Edermath Orchard. Bury the bodies there.”

With a few exaggerated sighs, the party trudged across the road and around the Lionshield Coster to the Town Green, attracting the glances of townsfolk out and about running errands. There were some raised eyebrows, as a group of armed people carrying bodies was unusual, but some recognized a bit of red cloak not completely stored in a backpack, and gave nods of acknowledgement.

The rough ground beyond the Town Green had a few grave markers here and there. John’s shovel was put to good use, as the whole party worked together to dig a hole deep enough for four bodies, which were unceremoniously dumped in and buried. Brocc scrounged up rocks to mark the spot, “I don’t want some poor townsperson to accidentally dig them up,” he said as he distributed the rocks in a meaningless, but unmistakably deliberate, arrangement.

That done, the party turned to go back into town, with a few options before them: 

1. Talk to Daran Edermath, to discover what he knows about the area given his history as an adventurer
2. Have Pip take them to Carp, who supposedly knows of a tunnel that could be a secret entrance to the hideout
3. Go to Barthen Provisions to see what Barthen knows and restock basic gear.
4. Go to the Lionshield Coster to see what Linene knows. Linene is the only source of weapons and armor in town other than the blacksmith, and most of what you could get from him was either crafted from scratch or repaired. She was picky about who she sold equipment to, and so may have had a run-in or two with the group.
5. Go to the blacksmith to see if he’s had any unusual requests, especially if the Redbrands are unable to get what they need from Linene.
